Should We Contact Aliens? B2/C1 Lesson

B2/C1 ESL Connect Video lesson based on the tapescript: SETI, METI, alien contact, risk, democratic decision-making and the Fermi paradox.

Open video on YouTube

Lesson Aim

Students watch a video about the search for extraterrestrial intelligence and debate whether humanity should listen quietly or actively send messages into space.

Video structure: Part 1 introduces the Fermi question, SETI and METI. Part 2 explores the risks of messaging aliens, the Zoo Hypothesis, language barriers and long timescales.

Before watching

  1. Do you think intelligent alien life probably exists somewhere in the universe?
  2. Should humans try to contact aliens, or is it safer to stay quiet?
  3. Who should have the right to send a message on behalf of Earth?

Thought-Provoking Questions

Discuss these questions in pairs or small groups. Give reasons, examples and possible consequences.

1. Should humanity actively send messages to alien civilisations, or simply listen?

2. Who has the right to speak for Earth?

3. Is Stephen Hawking’s warning realistic caution, or unnecessary fear?

4. If aliens already know we exist, does that change the ethical debate about sending messages?

5. What should a message from Earth contain, and what should it avoid?

6. Could communication across thousands of years ever create a meaningful relationship?

7. Would discovering alien life make humanity feel more united or more divided?

Challenge: Choose one question and prepare a one-minute answer using cautious language such as: It could be argued that..., One possible risk is..., This becomes more complicated because...

Context

Situation: An international space committee is debating whether Earth should send a new message to a potentially habitable planet. Student A supports METI. Student B supports SETI’s cautious approach.

Student A

Argue that humanity should reach out first and demonstrate intelligence and willingness to communicate.

Student B

Argue that sending messages is risky, undemocratic and scientifically uncertain.

Role play task: Discuss the proposal for five minutes. By the end, agree on one condition that must be met before Earth sends a message.
