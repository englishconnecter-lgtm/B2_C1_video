Fixed Mindset vs Growth Mindset

B2/C1 lesson on ability, feedback, failure, effort and improvement.

Open video on YouTube

Lesson Overview

Students watch a video about Carol Dweck’s idea of fixed and growth mindsets. The lesson explores ability, feedback, failure, effort, obstacles, creative risk-taking and continual improvement.

Before you watch

  1. Do you think talent is something people are born with, or something they can develop?
  2. How do you usually react when someone gives you critical feedback?
  3. Why do some people avoid difficult tasks even when they want to improve?

B2/C1 Grammar: Comparisons and Contrasts

This grammar section practises contrast language and comparative structures for comparing fixed and growth mindsets.

FunctionPatternExample
Probabilitymore/less likely to + verbPeople with a growth mindset are more likely to embrace challenges.
Contrastwhereas / whileA fixed mindset sees failure as permanent, whereas a growth mindset sees it as a chance to learn.
Cautious comparisonmay / might + verbSomeone with a fixed mindset may avoid feedback.
Comparison of behaviourmore likely to / less likely toPeople with a fixed mindset are less likely to take creative risks.
