Delegation at Work: B2/C1 Lesson

B2/C1 ESL Connect Video lesson based on a workplace dialogue: delegation, workload, client management, burnout, prioritising and leadership after promotion.

Video link: Open on YouTube

Lesson Aim

Students watch a workplace dialogue about a newly promoted employee who is overwhelmed because he refuses to delegate. The lesson focuses on workload management, client communication, burnout and leadership language.

Video structure: Part 1 shows John refusing help and taking on too much. Part 2 shows the consequences and introduces the need for delegation.

Before watching

  1. Why do some employees avoid delegating tasks?
  2. What are the risks of saying “I’ll do it myself” too often?
  3. What changes when someone gets promoted?

B2/C1 Grammar: Delegation, Obligation and Regret

This video shows someone under pressure, so the grammar focus is on obligation, past regret and diplomatic workplace language.

Example: John should have delegated the report instead of trying to do everything himself.

Grammar Section 1: Obligation and Necessity

1. Complete: “John ___ call Mr Fujiyama back.”

2. Which sentence sounds professional?

Grammar Section 2: Past Regret

1. Complete: “John ___ more tasks earlier.”

2. Which sentence is correct?

Grammar Section 3: Diplomatic Workplace Language

1. Which sentence is most diplomatic?

2. Complete: “Perhaps we could look at which tasks ___ shared with the team.”

Thought-Provoking Questions

Discuss these questions in pairs or small groups. Give reasons, examples and possible consequences.

1. Why do some newly promoted employees try to do everything themselves?

2. Is refusing to delegate a sign of commitment or poor leadership?

3. How can a manager know when an employee is close to burnout?

4. What tasks should a manager never delegate?

5. How can delegation be done without making colleagues feel overloaded?

6. What is the difference between “getting things done” and leading a team effectively?

7. Should companies train people in delegation before promoting them?

Challenge: Choose one question and prepare a one-minute answer using cautious language such as: It could be argued that..., One possible concern is..., This becomes more complicated because...

Context

Situation: John has been promoted recently, but he is exhausted because he is trying to handle every task himself. His manager wants to help him delegate more effectively.

Student A

You are John. Explain why you have been doing everything yourself and what you are worried about delegating.

Student B

You are John’s manager. Be supportive but firm. Help him choose three tasks to delegate and one task to keep.

Role play task: Discuss the situation for five minutes. By the end, agree on a delegation plan for the next week.
