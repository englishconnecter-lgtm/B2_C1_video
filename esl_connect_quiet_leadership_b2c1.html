Quiet Leadership: Do We Need More Introverted Leaders?

B2/C1 video lesson on leadership, introversion, extroversion, listening and team culture.

Open video on YouTube

Lesson Overview

Students watch a short video about the “Extrovert Ideal” and the value of quieter leadership. The lesson explores whether great leaders need to be confident extroverts, or whether listening, humility and considered judgement are just as important.

Before you watch

  1. What qualities do people usually associate with great leaders?
  2. Can a quiet person be a strong leader?
  3. Do meetings sometimes reward people who speak quickly rather than people who think deeply?

Comprehension 2

Click each question to reveal the answer.

What strengths do introverts often bring to leadership?
They are more likely to listen, process team ideas, consider ideas deeply, be humble and credit their team.
Why may introverted leaders be less likely to compromise themselves?
Because their motivation often comes from within, so they may be less driven by rewards like money or power.
What percentage of employees may tend towards the quieter side?
Research suggests between 40 and 60% of employees may tend to the quieter, more introverted side.
Why can quieter employees feel invisible in meetings?
Because their thoughtful approach can make it difficult to answer on-the-spot questions quickly.
What is the final message about leadership?
Standing back and listening can empower leaders and followers, and leadership should allow different personality types to thrive.
