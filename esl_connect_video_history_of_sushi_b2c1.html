The History of Sushi: B2/C1 Lesson

B2/C1 ESL Connect Video lesson based on the tapescript: food history, cultural adaptation, Westernisation, globalisation and culinary craftsmanship.

Open video on YouTube

Lesson Aim

Students watch a video about the journey of sushi from a fish-preservation method to a global food trend and discuss how food changes when it moves across cultures.

Video structure: Part 1 explains sushi’s origins, fermentation and modernisation in Japan. Part 2 explores sushi’s global spread, Western adaptations, safety rules and modern sushi culture.

Before watching

  1. What foods from your country have changed after becoming popular abroad?
  2. Does adapting a traditional food make it more creative or less authentic?
  3. Why do some foods become global trends while others remain local?

Thought-Provoking Questions

Discuss these questions in pairs or small groups. Give reasons, examples and possible consequences.

1. When a traditional food is adapted for another culture, does it lose authenticity or gain a new identity?

2. Should foods like sushi be judged by tradition, taste, safety or creativity?

3. Why do some people become fanatical devotees of certain foods?

4. Is Westernised sushi still sushi?

5. How do health, convenience and exoticism help market a food globally?

6. Should restaurants clearly distinguish between traditional food and fusion food?

7. What local food from your country could become popular worldwide if it were adapted?

Challenge: Choose one question and prepare a one-minute answer using cautious language such as: It could be argued that..., One possible concern is..., This becomes more complicated because...

Context

Situation: A restaurant is planning a new sushi menu for international customers. Student A wants to keep the menu traditional. Student B wants to include Westernised and fusion options.

Student A

Defend traditional sushi. Explain why authenticity, skill and food culture matter.

Student B

Defend adaptation. Explain why local tastes, safety rules and creativity matter.

Role play task: Discuss the menu for five minutes. By the end, agree on three items: one traditional, one adapted and one fusion dish.
