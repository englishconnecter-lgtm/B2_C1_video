3D Food Printing: The Future of Cooking?

B2/C1 video lesson on food technology, software-controlled cooking and innovation.

Open video on YouTube

Lesson Overview

Students watch a video about 3D food printing, software-controlled cooking and laser cooking. The lesson explores how digital tools could change food design, textures, ingredients and the future of cooking.

Before you watch

  1. Would you eat food made by a 3D printer?
  2. What problems could digital cooking solve?
  3. Is food printing a gimmick or a serious innovation?

B2/C1 Grammar: Future Possibility and Innovation

This grammar section practises language for describing new technology and future possibilities.

FunctionPatternExample
Future possibilitycould / might + verbFood printers could create textures we cannot imagine.
Result of a conditionOnce + clause, you can...Once cooking is controlled by software, you can design new foods.
Purposeallow someone to + verbFood CAD allows non-programmers to design foods.
Contrastnot about..., but about...This is not about cooking faster, but about creating new food.

Discussion

  5. Would food CAD make cooking more creative or less human?
