Comprehension 1

Click each question to reveal the answer.

Why does language matter in luxury?
Because the words used with clients can elevate or diminish the entire experience.
Why does the speaker prefer “investment” to “price” or “cost”?
Because “investment” sounds more thoughtful and considered, while “price” can feel transactional.
Why is “contract” avoided in luxury communication?
Because it sounds formal, rigid and legal; words like “paperwork” or “agreement” feel softer and more collaborative.
What is the difference between “buy” and “own”?
“Own” feels like an accomplishment, while “buy” can feel like pressure.
What should every luxury interaction feel like?
It should feel as refined as the experience being offered.

Comprehension 2

Click each question to reveal the answer.

Why is “deposit” not ideal in luxury language?
Because it sounds ordinary and is associated with everyday payments, bills or hotel bookings.
What does “initial investment” suggest?
It suggests value, long-term worth and intentionality.
Why does the speaker avoid the word “selling”?
Because selling can feel pushy, as if the client is being convinced or pressured.
What is the difference between the two boutique examples?
The first focuses on price, selling and pressure; the second focuses on exclusivity, investment and acquiring something special.
Why should “objections” be reframed as “areas of concern”?
Because it creates a more open and respectful conversation where the client feels heard rather than pushed.

B2/C1 Grammar: Polite Reframing and Softening Language

This grammar section practises transforming direct or transactional language into refined, client-centred luxury communication.

FunctionPatternExample
Softening direct languageI can assist you in + -ingI can assist you in acquiring this piece today.
Reframing a problemI understand this as...I understand this as an area of concern for you.
Guiding the clientLet me walk you through...Let me walk you through the details.
Elevating a productThis piece is part of...This piece is part of a limited collection.
