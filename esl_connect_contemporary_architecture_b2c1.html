Contemporary Architecture: Beautiful or Brutal?

Lesson Overview

Students watch a short video about the controversy surrounding contemporary architecture. The lesson explores whether modern buildings are innovative, cold, beautiful, ugly, playful, functional or out of place.

Before you watch

  1. Do you prefer traditional or modern architecture?
  2. Can one building damage the visual identity of a city?
  3. Should architects design for beauty, function or originality?

Comprehension 1

Click each question to reveal the answer.

What contrast does the video make at the beginning?
It contrasts harmonious simplicity with cold sterility, and “less is more” with “less is bore”.
Why is the Tour Montparnasse controversial in Paris?
Because it is a tall modern skyscraper in a city famous for beautiful traditional architecture, so it looks out of place.
What happened two years after the Tour Montparnasse was completed?
Buildings over seven storeys were barred from construction in the city centre.
What was brutalism focused on?
It focused on shaping raw materials such as concrete into uncompromising, austere forms.
Why did many critics dislike brutalism?
They thought it ignored the human desire for warm, beautiful and inviting spaces.

How has modern or postmodern architecture changed in recent decades?
It has become more playful, experimenting with organic shapes and distorted planes.
What was the Selfridges Building supposed to do?

What concern does the video raise about unusual modern buildings?
It asks whether they can damage a city’s visual identity or become vanity projects.

People have to live inside buildings, and minimalism only goes so far in meeting human needs.

B2/C1 Grammar: Concession and Evaluation

This grammar section practises language for balanced opinions about controversial architecture.

FunctionPatternExample
ConcessionAlthough / Even though + clauseAlthough the building is innovative, it does not fit the area.
ContrastWhereas / While + clauseWhile traditional architecture can comfort us, modern design can challenge us.
EvaluationIt is in danger of + -ingThe design is in danger of becoming a vanity project.
Degree / limitationonly goes so farMinimalism only goes so far with matching human need.
