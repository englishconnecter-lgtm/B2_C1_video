Lesson Aim

Students watch a healthcare technology video about the Hugo robotic-assisted surgery system and discuss how hospitals choose innovation partners, improve patient access and train surgical teams.

Video structure: Part 1 focuses on vision, patient access and the impact of robotic-assisted surgery. Part 2 focuses on modular design, open consoles, training, partnership and long-term support.

Before watching

  1. How can technology improve patient outcomes in hospitals?
  2. What should hospitals consider before investing in expensive medical technology?
  3. What is the difference between simply buying equipment and building a true partnership?

Thought-Provoking Questions

Discuss these questions in pairs or small groups. Give reasons, examples and possible consequences.

1. Should hospitals invest in expensive robotic systems if only some patients can access them at first?

2. What matters more in healthcare innovation: better technology, better training or better partnerships?

3. Can a medical technology company become a true partner, or is it always mainly a supplier?

4. How might open-console robotic surgery change medical education?

5. What risks could hospitals face when relying heavily on one technology partner?

6. Does being “at the forefront” of technology always mean better patient care?

7. What does “the sky is the limit” mean in the context of robotic surgery, and are there any limits that should remain?

Challenge: Choose one question and prepare a one-minute answer using cautious language such as: It could be argued that..., One possible concern is..., This becomes more complicated because...

Context

Situation: A hospital board is deciding whether to invest in a new robotic surgery platform and enter a long-term partnership with a medical technology company.

Student A

You are a surgeon. Argue that the platform will improve access, training and patient outcomes.

Student B

You are a hospital manager. Ask about cost, training, risk, evidence and long-term support.

Role play task: Discuss the proposal for five minutes. By the end, agree on three conditions that must be met before the hospital signs the partnership.
