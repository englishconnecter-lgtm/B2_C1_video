Astronaut Teamwork Test

B2/C1 video lesson on intelligent teamwork, leadership, communication and problem-solving under pressure.

Open video on YouTube

Lesson Overview

Students watch a video about astronaut candidates completing a teamwork test underwater. The task seems simple — building a cube from seven pieces — but limited time, breath control, non-verbal communication and unclear roles make it extremely difficult.

Before you watch

  1. What makes a team work well under pressure?
  2. Is leadership more important than individual intelligence?
  3. What problems might occur if a team cannot communicate clearly?

B2/C1 Grammar: Past Modals for Reflection

This grammar section practises language for reflecting on poor teamwork and saying what should, could or might have happened differently.

FunctionPatternExample
Criticising past actionshould have + past participleThey should have talked more before starting.
Missed possibilitycould have + past participleThey could have distributed roles more clearly.
Uncertain past possibilitymight have + past participleThey might have done better with clearer signals.
Negative reflectionshouldn’t have + past participleMerritt shouldn’t have tried to solve it alone.
