Lesson Overview

Students watch a video about Dunbar’s Number: the idea that people can maintain around 150 meaningful social relationships, organised in layers of closeness.

Useful Patterns

PatternExample
about / around + numberWe can maintain around 150 relationships.
as many as + countable nounAs many as 150 people may be in this circle.
as much as + percentage / uncountable nounAs much as 40% of our time goes to close friends.
up to + maximumWe may recognise up to 1500 faces.
Task: Describe your own friendship circles using approximate numbers and quantifiers.

Thought-Provoking Questions

  1. Do you think 150 is a realistic friendship limit today?
  2. Has social media increased or weakened our friendship circles?
  3. Why do the five closest people matter so much?
  4. Is gossip always negative, or can it build relationships?
  5. What happens when people focus on quantity instead of quality?
  6. Can companies or schools use Dunbar’s Number to organise people better?

Role Play

Situation: A company is growing quickly and wants to organise its teams in a way that protects trust, communication and collaboration.

Student A: Organisational psychologist

Explain Dunbar’s Number and recommend team sizes, communication routines and ways to protect close working relationships.

Student B: Company leader

Ask how the theory applies to remote work, fast growth and large departments.
