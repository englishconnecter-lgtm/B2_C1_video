Comprehension 2

Click each question to reveal the answer.

Why should you talk about progress?

It helps your manager see how you have grown, what you have learned and how you have improved.

Why might your boss overlook your progress?

Managers are busy, so they may not notice everything you have achieved unless you make it clear.

How should you talk about weaknesses?

Mention one or two areas for improvement, explain your intention to improve, include a time frame and suggest development opportunities.

Why should you ask about future plans?

It shows interest in the bigger picture and helps you understand how your role connects to the department or company goals.

What should you walk away with after the review?

Three to five future expectations your boss has of you.

B2/C1 Grammar: Present Perfect and Review Language

Performance reviews often use the present perfect because you are connecting past work to the present conversation.

UsePatternExample
Achievements up to nowI have + past participleI have organised three webinars this year.
Progress over timeI have been + -ingI have been improving my time management.
Recent developmentSince my last review, I have...Since my last review, I have taken on more client responsibility.
Future goalIn the next 3–6 months, I would like to...In the next six months, I would like to lead a larger project.

PCOR Practice

Use the PCOR method to prepare a short performance-review answer.

PProject: What project or task did you work on?
CChallenge: What difficulty did you face?
OOvercome: What action did you take?
RResult: What positive result did you create?
Model: One achievement I’m proud of is organising a client webinar. The main challenge was coordinating several speakers with different schedules. I overcame this by creating a clear timeline, setting deadlines and checking in regularly with the team. As a result, the webinar ran smoothly and helped improve client engagement.

Situation: An employee is having a yearly review with their manager. The employee wants feedback, growth opportunities and clearer expectations.

Talk about one achievement using PCOR. Explain one area for improvement. Ask about department goals and future expectations.

Ask follow-up questions. Give feedback. Explain three future expectations and suggest one learning opportunity.
