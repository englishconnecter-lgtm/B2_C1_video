B2/C1 lesson on remote meetings, technical problems, polite troubleshooting and workplace communication.

Lesson Overview

Students watch a short comedy video about everything that can go wrong in an online meeting. The lesson focuses on video-call problems, troubleshooting, interruptions, meeting etiquette and useful language for remote work.

Before you watch

  1. What usually goes wrong in online meetings?
  2. How do you politely tell someone their microphone or sound is not working?
  3. When is it better to reschedule a meeting?

What final decision do they make?

B2/C1 Grammar: Polite Troubleshooting Language

This grammar section practises polite questions, suggestions and instructions for solving technical problems in online meetings.

FunctionPatternExample
Checking a problemCan you + verb?Can you hear me?
Giving a suggestionTry + -ingTry adjusting your output settings.
Making a requestCould you + verb?Could you put your headphones on?
Explaining a problemIt sounds like / It looks like...It sounds like your microphone is causing an echo.

Role Play: Online Meeting Disaster

Situation: A team is trying to hold an online meeting, but one person has no sound, another has an echo, someone is late, and someone else is calling from a noisy place.

Student A: Meeting Chair

Keep the meeting calm. Troubleshoot technical problems, ask people to use headphones and decide whether to continue or reschedule.

Student B: Team Member

You are having technical problems. Explain what is happening and ask for help politely.
