First Impressions: Why Bad Can Feel Stronger Than Good

B2/C1 psychology lesson on impression updating, negativity bias and social judgement.

Lesson Overview

Students watch a social psychology video about how we form and update first impressions. The lesson explores negativity bias, moral behaviour, competence, rare actions and how the brain processes new information about people.

Before you watch

  1. How quickly do you form first impressions of people?
  2. Is it easy for someone to change your first impression of them?
  3. Do bad actions affect our judgement more than good actions?

Comprehension 2

Click each question to reveal the answer.

What type of information usually has a strong impact on impressions?
Very negative, highly immoral information usually has a stronger impact than very positive, highly moral information.
Why does this negativity bias occur?
Because immoral behaviours are seen as more diagnostic or revealing of a person’s true character.
When does this bias flip?
When we judge abilities and competencies, positive information can be weighted more heavily.
What kind of behaviours do people weigh more heavily overall?
Behaviours that are less frequent, such as highly immoral actions or highly competent actions.
What does the brain seem to track?
It tracks how typical or unusual behaviours are in daily life to help judge other people’s character.
