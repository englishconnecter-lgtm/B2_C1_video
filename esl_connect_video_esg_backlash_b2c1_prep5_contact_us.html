Thought-Provoking Questions

  1. Should investment firms consider environmental and social issues?
  2. Does ESG distract companies from maximising returns?
  3. Can voluntary corporate action solve sustainability problems?
  4. Why has ESG become politically controversial?
  5. Should governments, not companies, lead climate policy?
  6. Could the term ESG disappear while the issues remain?

Role Play

Situation: An investment company is deciding whether to keep using ESG language in its client communications.

Student A: ESG investment manager

Defend ESG. Explain why sustainability risks matter for long-term business performance.

Student B: Client / board member

Challenge ESG. Ask about fiduciary duty, returns, politics and whether the term should be dropped.
