Creativity Is Learnable: B2/C1 Lesson

B2/C1 ESL Connect Video lesson based on the tapescript: creativity, curiosity, problem-framing, uncomfortable conversations and creative habits.

Open video on YouTube

Lesson Aim

Students watch a video about whether creativity is an innate talent or a learnable skill, then discuss how to create the conditions for original thinking.

Video structure: Part 1 challenges the idea of a “creative gene” and introduces problem-framing and curiosity. Part 2 explores boring tasks, idea storage, uncomfortable conversations and respecting creative moments.

Before watching

  1. Do you think creativity is something people are born with, or something they can develop?
  2. When do your best ideas usually appear?
  3. Can boring tasks or uncomfortable conversations make someone more creative?

Thought-Provoking Questions

Discuss these questions in pairs or small groups. Give reasons, examples and possible consequences.

1. Is creativity more like a talent, a habit or a process?

2. Can schools and workplaces teach creativity, or do they often limit it?

3. Why might boring or unfamiliar tasks sometimes lead to better ideas?

4. Should people deliberately seek conversations with people they disagree with?

5. Is creativity possible without empathy?

6. What kinds of environments make people less creative?

7. How can a person respect moments of inspiration without becoming distracted from daily responsibilities?

Challenge: Choose one question and prepare a one-minute answer using cautious language such as: It could be argued that..., One possible concern is..., This becomes more complicated because...

Context

Situation: A team needs to design a new community project but feels stuck. Student A wants a practical solution quickly. Student B wants to use Grenny’s creativity principles to generate better ideas.

Student A

Explain the problem, ask for practical ideas and challenge anything that feels too abstract.

Student B

Use the video’s principles: frame the problem, obey curiosity, consider unfamiliar perspectives and capture ideas.

Role play task: Discuss the project for five minutes. By the end, agree on one creative idea and one next step to test it.
