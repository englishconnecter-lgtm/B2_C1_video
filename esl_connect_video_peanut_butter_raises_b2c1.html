Peanut Butter Raises: B2/C1 Lesson

B2/C1 ESL Connect Video lesson based on the tapescript: compensation, across-the-board raises, merit pay, inflation, employee motivation and workplace fairness.

Video link: Open on YouTube

Lesson Aim

Students watch a news discussion about “peanut butter increases” and evaluate the advantages and disadvantages of giving identical raises to all employees instead of merit-based raises.

Video structure: Part 1 explains what peanut butter increases are and why companies use them. Part 2 looks at pay figures, inflation, top performers and alternative ways to recognise employees.

Before watching

  1. Should everyone in a company receive the same percentage raise?
  2. How should companies reward top performers?
  3. Can merit-based pay ever be completely fair?

B2/C1 Grammar: Comparison, Cause and Workplace Evaluation

This video compares pay systems and evaluates consequences, so the grammar focus is on comparative structures, cause-and-effect and balanced argument language.

Example: Although across-the-board raises are simpler to administer, they may reduce motivation among top performers.

Grammar Section 1: Comparatives

1. Complete: “Peanut butter raises are administratively ___ complex.”

2. Which sentence is correct?

Grammar Section 2: Cause and Result

1. Complete: “Because merit-based raises require individual reviews, they can be ___.”

2. Which sentence gives a clear result?

Grammar Section 3: Balanced Evaluation

1. Which sentence sounds most balanced?

2. Complete: “One possible drawback is ___ high performers may lose motivation.”

Thought-Provoking Questions

Discuss these questions in pairs or small groups. Give reasons, examples and possible consequences.

1. Are across-the-board raises fairer than merit-based raises?

2. Should companies prioritise simplicity, fairness or motivation when designing pay systems?

3. If managers are biased, is equal pay growth the best solution?

4. How should companies recognise employees who consistently go the extra mile?

5. Can bonuses and promotions fully compensate for identical annual raises?

6. Should inflation automatically influence annual pay increases?

7. Would you prefer a guaranteed equal raise or a chance at a larger merit-based raise?

Challenge: Choose one question and prepare a one-minute answer using cautious language such as: It could be argued that..., One possible drawback is..., This becomes more complicated because...

Context

Situation: A company is deciding whether to introduce peanut butter raises next year. HR wants simplicity and fairness. Team leaders worry about motivation and talent retention.

Student A

You are the HR director. Argue for across-the-board raises because they are fairer, simpler and less biased.

Student B

You are a team manager. Argue that top performers need merit-based recognition, bonuses or promotions.

Role play task: Discuss the policy for five minutes. By the end, agree on a pay system that balances fairness, motivation and cost.
