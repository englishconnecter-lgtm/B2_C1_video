Useful Patterns

FunctionPatternExample
Adviceshould + base verbCompanies should invest in information.
Necessityhave to + base verbManagers have to understand regulation.
Strong necessitymust + base verbLeaders must adapt quickly.
Possibilitymight + base verbPrices might fluctuate suddenly.
Task: Give strategic advice to a company entering an unpredictable market using should, must, have to and might.

Thought-Provoking Questions

  1. Is long-term planning still useful in a VUCA world?
  2. Which VUCA element is most difficult for companies?
  3. Should companies stockpile inventory, or is that inefficient?
  4. How can leaders make decisions when there is no precedent?
  5. Can experiments reduce ambiguity in a new market?
  6. What skills do managers need to thrive in chaotic conditions?

Role Play

Situation: A company is planning to enter a new emerging market, but the leadership team is worried about VUCA conditions.

Student A: Strategy consultant

Explain the four VUCA risks and recommend practical actions: slack, information, specialists and experiments.

Student B: CEO

Ask questions about cost, speed, uncertainty and how to know whether the plan is working.
