Lesson Aim

Students watch a news report about the Air India–Vistara merger and practise language for antitrust regulation, market competition, air fares, airline strategy and aviation growth.

Before watching

  1. Why might regulators block or challenge a merger?
  2. What happens when only a few companies control a market?
  3. Why have air fares increased in some markets?

B2/C1 Grammar: Reported Speech for Business News

This section practises reported speech for summarising what regulators, experts and companies said in a business news report.

Useful language: The report said that... / The correspondent explained that... / Experts believed that... / The regulator asked whether...

Grammar Section 3: Useful Patterns

Direct speechReported speechUse
“The system is stressed.”He said that the system was stressed.Report a current situation.
“Air India might give up routes.”He explained that Air India might give up routes.Report possibility.
“Why are fares so high?”The presenter asked why fares were so high.Report information questions.
“The trend is visible.”The correspondent said that the trend was visible.Report analysis.
Task: Summarise the merger story using four reported-speech sentences: one about the regulator, one about possible concessions, one about air fares and one about Air India’s ambitions.

Thought-Provoking Questions

1. Should regulators block mergers that create very large companies?

2. Can mergers help customers if they create stronger airlines?

3. What is more important: lower fares or strong national airlines?

4. Why do air fares rise when demand grows faster than supply?

5. Should airlines give up routes to protect competition?

6. What risks come with two airlines controlling most of a market?

7. Could Air India become a global aviation powerhouse?
